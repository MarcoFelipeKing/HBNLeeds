Healthy Buildings Network Launch Event

26th October 2024

"By the time you are 70 you will have spent 63 years indoors..."

A healthy building supports both human health and environmental sustainability. It ensures clean air, comfortable conditions, natural light, and is free from harmful contaminants. The Healthy Buildings Network unites experts across disciplines to develop innovative, practical solutions for healthier built environments.

Event Schedule

  • 10:00 AM: Registration and Coffee
  • 10:30 AM: Welcome by Dr. Marco-Felipe King, Dr. Martín López-García, Dr. Irene Mussio
  • 11:05 AM: Keynote Addresses:
    • Dr. Henry Burridge - The value of research in monitoring and air quality interventions: schools as an exemplar
    • Prof. Cath Noakes - Healthy Buildings for Public Health
  • 12:00 PM: Lunch & Networking
  • 1:00 PM: Lightning Talks (Dr. Elisabeth Yang, Dr. Doug Booker, Dr. Hua Zhong, Dr. Philip Webb, Dr. Ailish Graham, Dr. Irene Mussio, Dr. Hilary Potter)
  • 2:15 PM: Coffee Break
  • 2:45 PM: Panel Discussion: "Interdisciplinary Approaches to Healthy Building Solutions" moderated by Dr. Doug Booker
  • 3:45 PM: Closing Remarks & Next Steps

Keynote Speakers

Dr. Henry Burridge (Imperial College London)

Dr. Burridge shared insights from the SAMHE project, emphasizing innovations in school air quality monitoring and implications for public health and building design.

Prof. Cath Noakes OBE (University of Leeds)

Prof. Noakes discussed how indoor air quality directly impacts public health, highlighting the role of building design in controlling airborne diseases, drawing from her extensive experience during the COVID-19 pandemic.

Panel Discussion

The panel explored how interdisciplinary collaboration can lead to innovative solutions for healthier buildings, featuring Jane Dickinson, Dr. Katy Wright, Dr. Paul Hutchings, and Dr. Henry Burridge, moderated by Dr. Doug Booker.
