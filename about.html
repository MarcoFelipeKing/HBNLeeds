About Healthy Buildings Network Leeds

Building bridges across disciplines, councils, and industries.

Our Story & Mission

Healthy Buildings Network Leeds emerged from a cross-disciplinary vision at the University of Leeds to foster meaningful collaboration on how buildings impact health, well-being, and sustainability. We actively engage across campuses, councils, industries, and communities—locally and globally.

Through seminars, workshops, funded projects, and dynamic partnerships, HBN drives innovative research and actionable insights for healthier indoor environments.

Meet the Founders

Martín López-García

Co-Founder & Director

Martín is an associate professor in the School of Mathematics and focuses on stochastic models for evaluating the spread of bacteria in hospital settings, analysing the role played by environmental contamination and hand-surface contact transmission.

Irene Mussio

Co-Founder & Director

Irene is a lecturer in the Leeds University Business School and specialises in behavioural economics and risk-risk trade-offs, translating her research into policy and practice.

Marco-Felipe King

Co-Founder & Director

Marco-Felipe is a lecturer in the School of Civil Engineering whose expertise includes modelling human behaviour in the built environment, focusing on infection transmission and how buildings promote or mitigate disease spread.
