About Us

Learn about our mission, our people, and our impact.

Our Story

The Healthy Buildings Network Leeds (HBN) emerged from a cross-disciplinary effort at the University of Leeds to explore how buildings impact human health, well-being, and sustainability—both locally in Yorkshire and worldwide. We bring together academics, industry partners, policymakers, and community members to tackle indoor environmental challenges through research, collaboration, and education.

Since our founding, HBN has hosted seminars featuring renowned experts—such as Liora, Chuck, and Pete Apps (Grenfell)—to engage diverse audiences. We also provide pump-priming grants that have already funded four innovative projects, pushing the boundaries of healthy building science.

Our Mission & Vision

Our mission is to create healthier indoor environments for everyone. We envision a future where building design and operations are driven by evidence-based approaches that promote physical and mental well-being, energy efficiency, and climate resilience.

Through collective expertise—spanning architecture, engineering, public health, environmental science, and social sciences—we strive to share knowledge, influence policy, and spark innovation that improves the quality of life in all built environments.
