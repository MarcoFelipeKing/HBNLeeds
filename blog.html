Pump-Priming Projects: Spotlight on Progress

Posted on February 20, 2025 by HBN Leeds

We’re thrilled to share an update on our four funded pump-priming projects, each exploring a unique angle on how building environments impact health and well-being. From air quality monitoring in historical structures to occupant-centric sensor feedback, these projects are already uncovering valuable insights.

The principal investigators recently hosted a collaborative “review and reflect” session, comparing lessons learned from each pilot study. Early results suggest that even modest interventions—such as improved ventilation strategies—can lead to tangible benefits in occupant comfort and reduced energy usage. Stay tuned for more details and final reports in the coming months!

The pump-priming initiative demonstrates our core commitment to sparking innovative healthy-building research at the University of Leeds and beyond. We can’t wait to see how these findings inform future policy, design, and technology in the built environment.

Seminar Recap: Liora, Chuck & Pete Apps (Grenfell)

Posted on January 10, 2025 by HBN Leeds

Our most recent seminar session welcomed Liora, Chuck, and Pete Apps (Grenfell), who brought vital perspectives on building safety and community resilience. They discussed lessons learned from the Grenfell tragedy, emphasizing the profound importance of regulatory oversight, occupant awareness, and empathy in the design and management of residential buildings.

A key takeaway was the urgent need for inclusive communication strategies that empower tenants and residents to voice concerns. Pete’s real-world examples showcased how transparency and accountability can prevent catastrophic outcomes. Liora and Chuck highlighted emerging technologies, including advanced smoke detection and fire-safe cladding materials, which could shape the future of building safety regulations.

If you missed this session, no worries—you can access a recording and supporting materials on our Seminars page. We encourage anyone interested in building safety and community well-being to check out this impactful discussion!
