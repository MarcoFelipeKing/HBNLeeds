Healthy Buildings Network Leeds

Hyper-local to global

Welcome to the Healthy Buildings Network Leeds, where we collaborate on research, innovation, and education for healthier, more sustainable buildings and their environments. Born out of a Horizons Challenge Network at the University of Leeds we empower collaborations both amongst disciplines that didn't previously talk to each other, and between academia, industry, and the public.

Join Us

What We Do

We connect experts, practitioners, and community members to explore how buildings impact health and well-being. Our activities include seminars, funded research projects, and collaborative initiatives to make indoor environments better for everyone.

What are Healthy Buildings?

Mental & Physical Health

Healthy buildings enhance physical and mental well-being by fostering supportive social, economic, emotional, and environmental indoor climates.

Sustainability

We prioritise sustainability throughout a building's lifecycle, from design to operation, focusing on resource usage, climate impact, energy efficiency, and responsible practices.

People

Empowering people with knowledge and means to act around healthy buildings through education and communication, promoting economic and environmental benefits.

Inclusive & Collaborative Design

Our approach ensures buildings are accessible, inclusive, and collaborative spaces, designed for diverse needs and community involvement.

Community Adaptation & Resilience

Healthy buildings support community resilience, emphasizing adaptive designs that enhance social cohesion, health, and long-term resilience.
